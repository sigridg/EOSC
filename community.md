## Overview

Building a thriving community around the Norwegian EOSC node is essential for its success. This section outlines our strategy for engaging researchers, institutions, and service providers across Norway's research ecosystem.

---

## Target Communities

### Primary Stakeholders

**Researchers & Research Groups**
- Early career researchers
- Established research groups
- Cross-disciplinary teams
- International collaborators based in Norway

**Research Institutions**
- Universities
- Research institutes
- University hospitals
- Independent research organizations

**Research Support Staff**
- Data stewards
- Research advisors
- IT infrastructure teams
- Library professionals

**Service Providers**
- Computing centers
- Data repositories
- Research software engineers
- Infrastructure operators

### Secondary Stakeholders

- Research funders (Research Council of Norway, etc.)
- Policy makers
- Industry partners
- Citizen science initiatives
- International EOSC community

---

## User Feedback & Co-Design

### Feedback Mechanisms

**Structured Feedback**
- Annual user survey
- Service-specific feedback forms
- Usability testing sessions
- Feature request voting system

**Ongoing Input**
- User advisory board (rotating membership)
- Office hours for direct feedback
- GitHub issues for technical feedback
- Community forum discussions

**Metrics We'll Track**
- User satisfaction scores
- Service adoption rates
- Support ticket trends
- Community engagement levels
- Training participation

### Co-Design Processes

**Involving Users in Development**
- Beta testing programs for new services
- User workshops for requirements gathering
- Pilot projects with research groups
- Student projects and internships

**Transparent Roadmap**
- Public roadmap showing planned features
- Voting on priorities
- Regular updates on progress
- Open retrospectives

## Champions & Advocates

### Champions

**Identifying Champions**
- Researchers actively using EOSC services
- Data stewards at institutions
- Technical staff supporting researchers
- Enthusiastic early adopters

**Supporting Champions**
- Recognition and visibility
- Priority access to new features
- Direct line to development team
- Speaking opportunities at events

**Champion Activities**
- Peer-to-peer support
- Creating tutorials and guides
- Presenting at their institutions
- Providing feedback to improve services

### Institutional Coordination

**Local Contacts at Institutions**
- Designated EOSC liaison at each major institution
- Regular coordination meetings
- Shared best practices
- Collaborative problem-solving

## International Connections

### European EOSC Community

**Participation in EOSC Forums**
- Regular attendance at EOSC symposia
- Contribution to working groups
- Knowledge sharing with other national nodes
- Collaborative projects

**Nordic Collaboration**
- Coordination with other Nordic EOSC initiatives
- Shared training resources
- Joint services where appropriate
- Regional best practice sharing

### Global Open Science Movement

- Connection to international open science networks
- Participation in global initiatives (RDA, CODATA, etc.)
- Sharing Norwegian perspectives and innovations
- Learning from international examples

## Communication Plan

### Key Messages

1. **For Researchers:** "EOSC makes your research easier, more collaborative, and more impactful"
2. **For Institutions:** "Join a national infrastructure that enhances Norway's research competitiveness"
3. **For Service Providers:** "Connect your services to a broader ecosystem and reach more users"
4. **For Funders:** "EOSC advances open science and maximizes research investment impact"
